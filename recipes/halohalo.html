Halo-halo

Halo-halo is a native dessert of the Philippines. It literally translates into "mix" or "mixed together". This dessert is very simple to serve. Crush some ice, add the ingredients, top it with "Leche Flan" and "Ube". It's notoriously famous since Philippines is a tropical country and it's always generally hot. That doesn't mean you can't eat it when it's cold though --trust me, we eat it even when it's raining.

Ingredients

  • 1 (12 ounce) can jackfruit, drained and diced
  • 1 (12 ounce) jar sweetened white kidney beans
  • 1 (12 ounce) jar red (adzuki) beans, drained
  • Shaved ice
  • ½ cup evaporated milk, or to taste
  • ½ cup vanilla ice cream, or to taste
  • Ube
  • Leche Flan (slice a portion)
  • (Optional) Corn bits, Nata de Coco, Coconut, Nuts

Steps

  1. Shave some ice. The finer, the better.
  2. Place jack fruit in a bowl. Add white beans and red beans and mix thoroughly.
  3. Place 2 tablespoons of the jackfruit mixture in a tall glass. Can add all the optional ingredients too.
  4. Fill glass 2/3-full of shaved ice. Pour evaporated milk over the shaved ice. Mix using a teaspoon. Top with 2 tablespoons vanilla ice cream, leche flan, and ube.
  5. Serve!
